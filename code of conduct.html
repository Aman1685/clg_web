Code of Conduct

The Government of India and the State Government of Haryana have strictly banned ragging in all professional institutions. As per official directives, stern disciplinary actions will be taken against any student found indulging in ragging activities.

Disciplinary measures may include:

  • Expulsion from the institute and hostels/mess
  • Imposition of fines and public apologies
  • Withholding scholarships or academic results
  • Debarring from events, campus placements, or industrial training

As per the Supreme Court of India, institutions must take immediate and effective measures to curb ragging. Violation of anti-ragging laws may lead to a fine of ₹25,000 and up to 3 years of imprisonment.

The institute actively monitors and prevents ragging by:

  • Deploying faculty members for vigilance, even during late hours
  • Seeking parental cooperation to ensure compliance
  • Briefing senior students about consequences and legal actions

Definition of Ragging:

Any disorderly conduct by words, actions, or written material that:

  • Causes psychological or physical harm to junior students
  • Generates a sense of fear, shame, or embarrassment
  • Induces students to perform acts they wouldn’t do ordinarily
  • Disrupts the peace or safety of the institution
